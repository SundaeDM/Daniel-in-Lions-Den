# Activities Page Brief — Daniel in the Lions' Den

---

## Page Purpose

Give families something fun and faith-enriching to do before or after the show — and capture email addresses in exchange for free downloadable content. This page keeps the show alive between the moment a parent discovers it and the moment they attend. It also serves as a re-engagement tool for families who've already seen the show.

**Primary CTA:** Download (free activity sheets — email gate optional)
**Secondary CTA:** Sign up for updates / email list growth

---

## Emotional Register

This is the **brightest, most playful page** on the site. The show is epic and dramatic — the Activities page is where kids themselves become the audience, not just parents making a purchasing decision. Energy should be warm, inviting, and slightly more lighthearted than the rest of the site. Think: a teacher opening a treasure chest.

This is also the page most likely to be revisited multiple times — once before the show, once after with the kids. Design for repeat use.

---

## Visual Brightness

**Predominantly light.** The hero header is dark (consistent with the rest of the site), but the activity grid and download cards live on warm parchment (`#FDF5E6`) and warm cream (`#F5EDD8`). Gold accents provide continuity with the rest of the brand. This is the most cream/parchment-heavy page — intentionally so.

Section rhythm: Dark hero → Light activity grid → Dark email signup → Light (optional app/bonus content) → Dark footer

---

## Section Inventory

### 1. PAGE HERO
- **Background:** Dark (`#2B1506`) with a lighter treatment than other page heroes — consider a warm radial glow from behind the headline to signal the transition to a brighter page
- **Content:**
  - Page title: "ACTIVITIES" in Cinzel, gold
  - Subhead: "Keep the adventure going long after the curtain falls." in Lato, cream
  - Below: optional illustration of a lion or small decorative motif (not a full photo — keeps this section light and quick)
- **Height:** Compact — 240px desktop, 180px mobile. Get to the activities fast.

### 2. INTRO LINE + CATEGORY FILTERS
- **Background:** Warm parchment (`#FDF5E6`)
- **Intro line:** "Download these free activities and bring the story of Daniel and Esther to life at home." (Lato, dark, centered, max-width 600px)
- **Filter pills (optional):** `All` · `Coloring Pages` · `Games & Puzzles` · `Bible Learning` · `Crafts`
  - Pill style: warm tan border on inactive, gold fill + dark text on active
  - This filter is a nice-to-have; include if there are 8+ activities. Omit if fewer.

### 3. ACTIVITY DOWNLOAD GRID
- **Background:** Warm parchment (`#FDF5E6`), continuing from intro
- **Layout:** 3-column grid desktop, 2-column tablet, 2-column mobile
- **Each activity card:**
  - Thumbnail preview of the printable (portrait image, gold border, slight warm shadow)
  - Activity name in Cinzel, dark (`#1A0900`), 16px
  - Short description in Lato, 14px, muted warm brown (`#6B4C2A`), 1–2 lines
  - "FREE DOWNLOAD" button — gold fill, dark text, pill shape
  - Small tag label (e.g., "Coloring Page" / "Puzzle" / "Memory Verse") in small muted all-caps above the title
- **Card hover:** Slight scale (1.02) + gold border brightens to `#F0B429`
- **Card style:** White/parchment fill, 1px gold border (`#C8860A`), 8px border-radius (slightly more rounded than the rest of the site — this page is warmer and more approachable), warm shadow

### 4. EMAIL GATE BEHAVIOR
- **Option A (soft gate):** Activities download immediately on click. At the bottom of the page, an email signup says: "Want more free activities and be first to hear about new shows? Join our list." No gate.
- **Option B (light gate):** Clicking "FREE DOWNLOAD" triggers a small modal: "Where should we send your download?" — email field + "SEND MY DOWNLOAD" button. After submitting, the file downloads automatically and a confirmation message appears. No password, no account creation.
- **Recommendation:** Option B (light gate) for email list growth, but modal must be fast and frictionless. Never ask for more than email + first name.
- **Modal style:** Dark overlay, centered card in warm parchment, Cinzel headline "Your free activity is ready!", gold button

### 5. EMAIL / UPDATES SIGNUP (STANDALONE SECTION)
- **Background:** Dark (`#2B1506`) — contrast break after the light grid
- **Content:** Headline: "Get More Free Activities." Subhead: "We add new content regularly. Join our list and we'll send them straight to you." Email input + "SIGN ME UP" button (gold fill).
- **Visual:** Consider a small lion illustration or golden paw print watermark at low opacity in the background

### 6. APP OR BONUS CONTENT (OPTIONAL)
- Only include if there is a companion app or digital experience. If not applicable, omit entirely — do not add a placeholder.
- If included: Light parchment background, two-column layout, app screenshot left, description + App Store / Play Store badges right

### 7. FOOTER
- Standard dark footer.

## Mobile Behavior

- Activity grid: 2-column on mobile (card padding reduces to 12px)
- Download modal: full-screen on mobile, not a floating modal
- Filter pills: horizontally scrollable, no wrapping
- Cards: thumbnail stays portrait aspect ratio; text stays readable at 14px minimum

---

## Forbidden

- No paid activities or premium content tier — everything is free
- No external links to other activity sites or Pinterest boards
- No auto-download behavior (always require a click)
- No "print from browser" instructions — just provide clean PDFs
- No craft activities that require adult supervision warnings in fine print (keep it family-self-sufficient)
- No "coming soon" placeholder cards — if an activity doesn't exist yet, omit the card

## Prompt Addendum (for Claude Design)

When building the Activities page:
- This is the most parchment-heavy page — lean into the warm light palette here intentionally
- Card border-radius is slightly higher (8px) than the rest of the site (4px) — this page is warmer and more approachable for kids
- The activity thumbnail previews are the visual stars of this page — make sure the cards give the thumbnails enough room (at least 180px height on the preview image)
- The download modal must feel fast and unobtrusive — no full-page overlay, no multi-step form
- Lion and parchment motifs (texture, paw prints, rope/scroll decorative elements) are appropriate here in a way they wouldn't be on the Schedule or FAQ pages
