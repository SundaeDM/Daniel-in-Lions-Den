# About the Show Page Brief — Daniel in the Lions' Den

---

## Page Purpose

Convert a curious parent into a confident buyer. A parent who lands here is asking: "Is this show worth the money? Is it right for my kids? What will we actually experience?" This page must answer all three before they leave. It is the second most important sales page after the homepage.

**Primary CTA:** Buy Tickets (repeated 2–3 times across the page)
**Secondary goal:** Build emotional investment in the story of Daniel and Esther before they ever arrive at the show

---

## Emotional Register

This page is the warmest, most story-driven page on the site. It should feel like you're being let into something special — not sold at. The tone: wonder and warmth. Think of how a Sight & Sound Theatre "About" page builds anticipation for a biblical story without over-explaining or being preachy.

---

## Visual Brightness

The "About the Show" page uses the **most light** of any page on the site. Long-form reading sections (story descriptions, character bios) live on warm parchment (`#FDF5E6`) for comfort. The hero is dark and dramatic. The gallery returns to dark. The overall rhythm: dark hero → light story sections → dark gallery → light specs → dark CTA.

---

## Section Inventory

### 1. PAGE HERO
- **Background:** Full-width show key art (Daniel + lions + cave light beam). Same poster imagery as homepage, but cropped/composed differently — perhaps wider, more panoramic. Dark overlay for legibility.
- **Content:** Page title "ABOUT THE SHOW" in Cinzel, gold. No additional copy — let the image speak. Height: roughly 60vh desktop.
- **Scroll indicator:** Subtle animated down-arrow in gold at bottom center of hero

### 2. THE STORY OF DANIEL
- **Background:** Warm parchment (`#FDF5E6`)
- **Layout:** Centered, single-column, max-width ~720px for comfortable reading
- **Headline:** "A Story of Courage That Changed the World." (Cinzel, gold, centered)
- **Body copy (3–4 paragraphs, ~60 words each):**
  - Para 1: Daniel's world — taken from his home, brought to a foreign kingdom, asked to abandon everything he believed. Sets the stakes.
  - Para 2: The lions' den — the king's decree, Daniel's choice, the miraculous night.
  - Para 3: What children take away — courage, faith, that one person standing firm can change a story.
  - Para 4: Brief transition to Esther. "And Daniel's story isn't the only one. Also featured: the extraordinary courage of Esther, the Bravest Queen."
- **Pull quote:** One short, bold quote in large Cinzel italic, gold, centered between paragraphs. Example: *"He was not afraid. He knew who held his future."* — presented as a narrative line, not a Bible verse citation (keep tone storybook, not theological)

### 3. ALSO FEATURING: ESTHER
- **Background:** Deep warm section — NOT dark cave black, but a rich deep burgundy-adjacent dark (`#2A0A18`) with the Esther rose accent palette
- **Layout:** Two-column: left = Esther character art (circular frame, gold border, matching poster cameo style), right = copy
- **Headline:** "Also Featuring the Story of Esther — The Bravest Queen" (Cinzel, Esther gold `#C4923A`)
- **Body copy (2 paragraphs):** Who Esther was. What she risked. What children see in her bravery. (~50 words each)
- **Tone:** Treat her as a co-equal story, not a footnote. Parents of daughters especially respond to Esther.
- **CTA:** None in this section — this is story-building, not selling yet

### 4. MEET THE CHARACTERS
- **Background:** Warm parchment (`#FDF5E6`)
- **Headline:** "Meet the Characters" (Cinzel, gold)
- **Layout:** Horizontal row of character cards (3–4 cards). Each card:
  - Character illustration / show art (portrait orientation)
  - Gold decorative border on card
  - Name in Cinzel, gold
  - 2-sentence description in Lato
  - Cards have a slight warm shadow (`rgba(200,134,10,0.12)`) — no cold grey shadows
- **Characters to include (minimum):** Daniel, A Lion (give them personality — they're characters, not props), Esther, King Darius (or relevant antagonist/authority figure)
- **Note:** Actual character art from the show should replace placeholders once available. Until then, use the CGI poster characters.

### 5. WHAT TO EXPECT (PRODUCTION SPECS)
- **Background:** Dark (`#2B1506`) — contrast shift
- **Layout:** Centered headline + 4-column icon grid
- **Headline:** "What to Expect" (Cinzel, gold)
- **Four spec tiles (icon + label + value):**
  1. 🎭 **Show Length** — 90 minutes (no intermission)
  2. 👏 **Audience Participation** — Yes — bring your energy!
  3. 👨‍👩‍👧 **Recommended Ages** — 3 and up (great for the whole family)
  4. ✨ **Special Effects** — Live music, dramatic lighting, and stage magic
- **Tile style:** Dark card (`#1A0B02`), gold icon/accent, cream text, gold 1px border, warm glow shadow
- **Below the grid:** One line in smaller muted text: "Sensory note: The show includes loud sounds and dramatic lighting effects. Ear protection recommended for sensory-sensitive guests."

### 6. GALLERY
- **Background:** Deep dark (`#0D0600`)
- **Headline:** "GALLERY" (Cinzel, gold, centered)
- **Layout:** Masonry or 3×2 grid of production photos / show art. Images edge-to-edge with thin gold gap between them (4px gap, `--color-gold-primary` as background showing between)
- **On hover:** Subtle scale (1.02) + warm overlay fade
- **Image count:** 6 minimum on desktop. Mobile shows 4 in 2×2.
- **Lightbox:** Clicking an image opens a dark-background lightbox with gold close button and left/right navigation arrows

### 7. BUY TICKETS CTA BLOCK
- **Background:** Warm spotlight effect — use a radial gradient from warm gold center (`rgba(245,196,74,0.15)`) outward to dark (`#0D0600`). This mimics the poster's light beam and is the most emotionally resonant CTA on the site.
- **Content:** Show logo lockup (small). Tagline: "The greatest story. Live on stage." Large gold "BUY TICKETS" button, centered. Below it in muted text: "Select your city on the next page."
- **Height:** Generous — minimum 320px. Let it breathe.

### 8. FOOTER
- Standard dark footer.

---

## Mobile Behavior

- Hero: 50vh height, title readable above fold
- Character cards: horizontally scrollable row on mobile (no wrapping to multi-row)
- Production specs: 2×2 grid on mobile
- Gallery: 2×2 grid (4 images), "View all" link to show remaining
- Lightbox: full-screen on mobile, swipe navigation

---

## Forbidden

- No cast/performer bios or headshots (this is about the story and show, not the production company)
- No video autoplay (a preview trailer is acceptable but must require user click)
- No review or testimonial section on this page (that belongs on the homepage if anywhere)
- No "About Us" / company history copy — this page is about the show, not the producer
- No bullet-point synopsis of the Bible story — write it as narrative, not a summary

## Prompt Addendum (for Claude Design)

When building the About the Show page:
- The light/dark rhythm is the most varied on this page — follow the section-by-section map strictly
- The Esther section has its own accent palette (`#7A1F3D` bg, `#C4923A` gold) — don't apply the main gold palette there
- The CTA spotlight effect is the emotional peak of the page — invest design care here
- Pull quotes should be visually distinct: large Cinzel italic, centered, gold, with thin horizontal rules above and below
