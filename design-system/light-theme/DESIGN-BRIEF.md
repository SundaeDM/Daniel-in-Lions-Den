# Daniel in the Lions' Den — Website Design Brief (Light / Warm Storybook Theme)
*Alternate to the Dark Premium theme. For use with Claude Design / v0 / Lovable / Cursor.*

---

## 1. Project Overview

**Show name:** Daniel in the Lions' Den
**Subtitle:** A Live Musical Experience
**Secondary story featured:** Esther — The Bravest Queen
**Show type:** Live-action children's stage play with musical elements
**Target market:** Christian families, churches, homeschool groups

---

## 2. Site Purpose (One Sentence)

Drive parents and families to buy tickets to live performances of *Daniel in the Lions' Den* through a warm, wonder-filled experience that feels like opening a beloved children's Bible — inviting, joyful, and full of life.

---

## 3. Primary Role

**Conversion engine** — ticket sales above everything else. Secondary role: credibility anchor. Same as the dark theme in function; entirely different in emotional register.

---

## 4. How This Theme Differs from the Dark Premium Theme

The dark theme leads with **awe and spectacle** — the show as epic event. This theme leads with **warmth and belonging** — the show as family memory in the making.

| Dark Premium | Warm Storybook |
|---|---|
| Cave darkness with light breaking in | Sunlit parchment with warm shadow for depth |
| Drama, spectacle, epic scale | Wonder, joy, invitation |
| Gold on deep black | Amber and sage on cream |
| Parent feels: "I have to take my kids to this" | Parent feels: "This was made for my family" |
| Lions are fearsome and majestic | Lions are warm, characterful companions |
| Daniel's courage is tested under fire | Daniel's courage is celebrated and joyful |

Neither is wrong — they serve different parents. The dark theme converts the parent who responds to spectacle; this theme converts the parent who responds to warmth.

---

## 5. Target Audience — Behaviors (Same as Dark Theme, Different Lens)

- **What they misunderstand:** They may expect a dry, preachy church event — not a joyful, high-energy family show. The site must radiate celebration.
- **Where they hesitate:** "Will my kids find this boring? Is it too 'religious' in a way that won't engage them?"
- **What this theme does differently:** It leads with the children's experience, not the production's scale. The parent sees the show through their child's eyes first.
- **The emotional promise:** "Your family will leave smiling, talking about it the whole way home, and asking to come back."

---

## 6. Visual Style

**Named style: Warm Storybook**

References:
- A beautifully illustrated children's Bible (soft watercolor backgrounds, warm ink illustrations)
- VeggieTales meets Pixar's visual warmth
- A well-designed picture book brought to life as a website

**Key visual principles:**
- Cream and parchment are the dominant backgrounds — the page feels like a page
- Rich warm colors (amber, burnt sienna, sage green) provide depth and accent
- Illustrations and decorative motifs (rope borders, scroll flourishes, paw prints, stars) add texture
- The lions read as warm, friendly companions — not predators
- Light comes from everywhere, not from a dramatic single beam — diffused, golden, like afternoon sun through a window
- Typography leans storybook: Playfair Display or Cinzel for headlines, rounded humanist sans for body

## 8. Typography

**Display headlines (H1, H2):** `Playfair Display` (Google Fonts), bold weight. Warm amber `#8B4A0A`. Title case (not all-caps as in the dark theme — all-caps reads dramatic; title case reads storybook).

**Section headlines (H2):** `Playfair Display`, semibold. Can include a hand-drawn underline SVG or a decorative rope/vine divider below.

**Body text:** `Nunito` or `Lato`, 17px, `#2C1A08`. Nunito's rounded terminals give a friendly, approachable feel better suited to this theme than the dark theme's Lato.

**CTA labels:** `Nunito`, bold, title case. Dark `#2C1A08` on gold button.

**Decorative accent text (pullquotes, callouts):** `Playfair Display` italic, amber, large (28–36px).

**Avoid:** Cinzel's sharp geometric serifs work for the dark epic theme but read slightly formal here. Playfair Display is warmer and more storybook. Either works — Playfair is preferred for this theme.

---

## 9. Decorative Language

This theme uses illustrative motifs that the dark theme does not. Use sparingly but deliberately:

- **Rope or twine borders** — thin decorative borders on section dividers and card edges
- **Paw print watermarks** — very low opacity (4–6%) as section background texture
- **Scroll/manuscript flourishes** — small decorative elements flanking section headlines
- **Stars** — small five-point stars used as bullet points or decorative spacers
- **Leaf/vine accents** — thin sage green vine motifs alongside the Esther section
- **Sun rays** — warm radial lines behind hero elements, evoking the light of deliverance

These should feel like a children's illustrated book, not clipart. SVG-drawn, clean, intentional.

## 10. Site Architecture

Same five pages as the dark theme:

| Page | URL slug | Primary CTA |
|---|---|---|
| Home | `/` | Buy Tickets |
| Schedule | `/schedule` | Ticket links per show date |
| About the Show | `/about` | Buy Tickets |
| Activities | `/activities` | Download (free) |
| FAQ | `/faq` | Buy Tickets (sticky) |

**Global nav:**
- Background: warm cream `#FDF5E6` with a subtle 1px warm sand border bottom
- Logo: left
- Nav links: center, `#2C1A08`, Nunito medium. Hover: amber underline
- CTA: right — "BUY TICKETS" pill button, gold fill `#F0B429`, dark text
- Mobile: hamburger menu; overlay uses warm parchment background, large links, centered

**Note:** No ticker/scrolling banner at the top (that read as urgent/promotional in the dark theme). This theme lets the nav breathe.

## 11. Homepage Section Inventory

### 1. HERO
- **Background:** Warm parchment with a soft radial warm-gold glow emanating from behind the key art (not a dark overlay — the image is bright and warm). If using the show poster, use a version with a warm cream/amber tone graded background rather than the cave dark.
- **Content:** Show title lockup — "Daniel in the Lions' Den" in Playfair Display, deep amber. "A Live Musical Experience" in smaller Nunito, muted warm brown. Two CTAs: "BUY TICKETS" (gold fill) and "SEE SHOW DATES" (amber outlined).
- **Key art treatment:** The CGI poster characters (Daniel + lions) but cropped or composited to feel warm and inviting — warm light wrapping them rather than dramatic cave darkness. If a separate "bright" key art version exists, use it. If not, use a warm color grade.
- **Height:** Full viewport.
- **Decorative detail:** Small star motifs or a subtle rope border at the bottom edge of the hero transitioning to the next section.

### 2. SHOW INTRO
- **Background:** Cream (`#FDF5E6`)
- **Layout:** Centered, max-width 680px
- **Content:** 2–3 sentence description of the show, warm and celebratory tone. Below: stat bar with 3 items (Show Length · Audience Participation · Ages). Icons in gold/amber, labels in warm brown.
- **Decorative:** Thin decorative scroll flourish above the paragraph

### 3. UPCOMING SHOWS
- **Background:** Warm parchment (`#F5EDD8`) — subtle shift from cream
- **Headline:** "Coming to a City Near You" (Playfair Display, amber) — note: not "UPCOMING SHOWS IN YOUR AREA" as in the dark theme; this headline is warmer and more inviting
- **Show rows:** Same structure (Date | City | Venue | Buttons) but on a warm sand card (`#EAD9B8`) with amber text, gold buttons, rope-style border on each card
- **"SEE ALL DATES" link:** Below the list, amber text, arrow icon

### 4. ABOUT THE STORY
- **Background:** Cream (`#FDF5E6`)
- **Layout:** Two-column. Left: show art (warm-toned). Right: copy.
- **Tone:** Story-forward, wonder-filled. "Long ago, a young man named Daniel made a choice that changed everything..."
- **Esther callout:** Small decorative badge or framed callout within this section: "Also featuring: Esther — The Bravest Queen." Rose/blush tones.

### 5. "WHY FAMILIES LOVE IT" — SOCIAL PROOF
- **Background:** Sage green section (`#5E7A52` bg with cream text) — the one deeply saturated section on the page, used intentionally for contrast and warmth
- **Content:** 3 short parent quotes (1–2 sentences each) in Playfair italic, cream. No star ratings — just human words.
- **Attribution:** First name + city only. ("— Sarah, Nashville TN")
- **Note:** This section replaces the Gallery on the homepage. Gallery moves to the About page where it has more context. Testimonials here build trust at the point in the scroll where parents are considering whether to buy.

### 6. GALLERY (CONDENSED)
- **Background:** Cream (`#FDF5E6`)
- **Layout:** A horizontal 4-image scroll or 2×2 grid — a preview, not a full gallery. "See more on the About the Show page" link below.
- **Image treatment:** Warm-toned, bright production photography. No dark overlays.

### 7. EMAIL SIGNUP
- **Background:** Gold accent section — warm amber/gold (`#C8860A`) background with cream text. The only section with a rich gold background (as opposed to gold accents on cream). Feels celebratory.
- **Content:** Headline: "Be the First to Know." Subhead: "Get ticket alerts, free activities, and show surprises straight to your inbox." Email input (cream fill) + "COUNT ME IN" button (dark fill, cream text).

### 8. FOOTER
- **Background:** Rich warm dark (`#2C1A08`) — the only near-dark section on the homepage outside the hero
- **Content:** Logo (light version on dark), nav links in warm cream muted, social icons, legal links, copyright
- **Decorative:** Thin gold rule above footer

## 13. Things to Avoid (Global)

- No dark cave backgrounds on any section except the footer
- No dramatic vignette effects or spot-lighting
- No metallic/chrome textures — warmth not glamour
- No heavy drop shadows (use warm ambient shadows: `rgba(44,26,8,0.1)`)
- No all-caps headlines (except CTA buttons) — title case reads warmer
- No urgent language ("LIMITED TIME," "SELLING FAST") — this theme trusts the story to sell
- No cold greys anywhere in the palette
- No modern SaaS design patterns (gradients, glassmorphism, dark-mode cards)

## 14. Mobile-First Notes

- Hero: title + CTA above fold on 390px. Warm key art visible but not dominant.
- Nav: hamburger, parchment overlay, large warm links
- Show rows: stacked card format, full-width gold CTA button
- Testimonials: single quote visible, swipe for more (or stack on mobile)
